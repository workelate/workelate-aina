Receipts over decks: what we hand you instead of slides

Ask a consultancy how the programme is going and you get a deck. Ask what changed and you get a workstream update. Neither is a receipt. A receipt is a number that was one thing before we arrived and is a different thing now, and that you can check yourself without taking our word for it.

At RockProsUSA the receipts read: dispatch cycle time down 38%, 2,140 invoices processed with zero manual touches, 11,200 operations hours returned in year one. Those are not projections from a business case. They are what the operation did after cutover.

A status deck is a claim. A number that moved is a receipt. The difference is who has to be trusted.

Nobody grades their own homework

During delivery our own agents write most of the volume code, which raises the obvious question: who checks the agents? The answer is deterministic verification. Every build runs inside gates a script enforces, not a person: does the system pass its behavioral probes on real client data, do the numbers reconcile end to end, does the interface hold up under the budgets we committed to. "Done" is not a feeling or a standup update. It is an exit code. If the gate fails, the work is not done, whoever, or whatever, claims otherwise.

We run the same gate on ourselves at the level you would least expect: our own status claims. When we tell a client "the invoices matched," that statement gets checked against the system before it goes out. It sounds paranoid until you have watched one wrong "it works" cost a client a week of quiet damage.

Why this is the whole trust story

A two-person firm shipping enterprise systems has no brand armor. We cannot say "trust us, we're a big four." What we can say is: here is the number before, here is the number now, here is the gate the build had to pass, and here is the operation running on it today. That is a stronger guarantee than a logo, and unlike a logo it compounds. Every shipped system is evidence the next client can check.

If a vendor pitches you AI automation and cannot name a single number that moved on a live system, you are not buying automation. You are buying a demo. Ours come with the numbers.
